# MABW v0.7.4 — Process Accountability Hardening

MABW is an open-source, contract-governed workflow for AI-assisted business briefings. This release tightens the process-accountability surface after a real private dogfooding run exposed a clear boundary: MABW can preserve the trace of how a claim entered and moved through the workflow, but v0.7.x does not yet prove that every source semantically supports every sub-claim.

## What's New

- **Stale audit reports now block finalize.** `finalize` rejects audit reports that still mention claim IDs absent from the current Claim Ledger, carry blocking findings, request repair, or carry stale ledger/brief binding metadata.
- **Source appendix delivery semantics are clarified.** The reader delivery bundle remains `output/delivery/brief.md` plus the configured DOCX. When source appendix output is configured, the appendix is appended inside those delivery files; standalone `output/source_appendix.md` remains an audit/control copy.
- **Release public-safety checks are wired in.** `check_release_consistency.py` now runs the tracked-file public-safety scan, including local-path, environment-file, token-like, and configured private-term checks.
- **A public failure study is included.** The organoid-industry case study shows how a readable brief can still overstate source support, and why v0.8 focuses on source-to-claim semantic support calibration.

## What This Release Does Not Claim

MABW v0.7.4 does not claim output-quality improvement, autonomous learning, semantic proof, strict fact-checking, or replacement of human review.

The precise claim is narrower:

> Every material claim links to a registered source entry: traceability, not yet semantic proof.

```bash

cd multi-agent-brief-workflow

bash scripts/demo.sh
bash scripts/demo-deep-dive.sh
```

- `docs/launch-validation.zh-CN.md`

Installer assets for other distribution paths exist in the repository, but they are not the primary v0.7.4 get-started path.
